Welcome to Chessweeper!

This tutorial will guide you through the basics of playing Chessweeper. Let's get started!

Setting Up the Board

First, you'll need to arrange your chess pieces on the board. Drag and drop the pieces to your desired starting positions. For this tutorial, we'll use a standard chess setup as an example.

Once you're happy with your setup, click the "End Turn" button to confirm your initial piece placement.

Starting the Game

After setting up, the game begins! If you're playing as Black, you might need to wait briefly for White to make their first move.

Making Moves

During your turn, you can move one piece. To do this, simply drag the piece you want to move to a valid destination square.

Hidden tiles

Unlike standard chess, your opponent's pieces are initially hidden from view. However, you can get clues about their locations:

  • Radar: Hover your mouse over your own pieces. You'll see a number indicating how many enemy pieces are within their moving range.
  • King's Location: The location of the opponent's King is always visible, as capturing it is the primary objective.

In the image above, the highlighted squares represent the radar of the selected piece. The green number shows how many enemy pieces are under those highlighted squares.

Claiming Tiles

You can click on unoccupied, hidden tiles to claim them. However, be cautious! If an enemy piece is hiding beneath a tile you click on, you will lose.

Movement Modes: Attack vs. Normal

When moving a piece onto a hidden square, you have two distinct modes, controlled by buttons at the top of the screen:

  • Attack Mode (Knife Icon): Intended for capturing potentially hidden enemies.
  • Normal Mode (Magnifying Glass Icon): Used for safely exploring and claiming tiles.

Understanding Movement Modes

Normal Mode: If you move onto a hidden tile and it's empty, you claim the tile and move there safely. If an enemy piece is present, you lose the game.

Attack Mode: If you move onto a hidden tile and it contains an enemy piece, you capture that piece, claim the tile, and move there. However, if the tile is empty, you lose the piece you moved.

Winning the Game

The ultimate goal of Chessweeper is to capture the opponent's King. Strategically use your movement and tile-claiming abilities to locate and capture it!

Good luck and have fun playing Chessweeper!
